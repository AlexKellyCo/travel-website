ITALY

Art

In Florence I visited the Galleria dell' Accademia which is where the Statue of David by Michelangelo is. The statue is much bigger than I thought it would be and it towered over everyone in the room. It was in a room by itself and there were some art students sketching near it but staring at art isn’t really my thing.

Architecture

In Florence I was able to see some canals which I thought looked nice to ride through since it was the middle of summer and extremely hot outside. I also got see some square-shaped buildings tucked into the side of mountains in the Italian countryside which looked beautiful yet dangerous.

Food

I ate plenty of pasta while in Italy. Most of the pasta dishes I ate were rich and covered in a creamy, cheesy sauce. I also tried plenty of gelato from street vendors and yet I still don’t really know what gelato actually is. Overall, the food was great, unless you’re trying to stick to a diet.

Pompeii

I visited the destroyed city of Pompeii. It was a surreal experience because it was a place I had learned about in my art history classes so it was more of a concept to me than a reality. It seemed so advanced for something built so long ago, though a lot of old places feel like that. There were people preserved in ash and placed in glass boxes that tourists could look at it. I didn’t like that part of the experience.

Cannes

I visited the city of Cannes which is where the famous film festival is held every year. I went to a perfume factory there and mixed my own perfume. I went for floral scents and ended up naming my creation, “Juno’s Glory.” The name has no meaning but I thought it sounded nice. It had to sit in darkness for a few weeks before I got the chance to smell the final product and now I keep the bottle as a decorative item on my shelf back home.
